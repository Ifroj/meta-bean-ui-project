Guardians of the Nation: Indian Army

The Indian Army, the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces, stands as a symbol of courage, discipline, and patriotism. With a rich history spanning centuries, it continues to safeguard the nation's sovereignty and territorial integrity.

Rich History & Legacy

1757

East India Company Army

The foundation of the modern Indian Army traces back to the British East India Company's forces, which later became the British Indian Army.

1857

First War of Independence

Indian soldiers played crucial roles, marking early resistance against colonial rule and setting the stage for future independence.

1947

Independence & Partition

The Indian Army was divided between India and Pakistan, facing the immediate challenge of the First Indo-Pak War.

1962

Sino-Indian War

The army faced challenges in high-altitude warfare, leading to significant modernization and restructuring.

1971

Liberation of Bangladesh

A decisive victory leading to the creation of Bangladesh, demonstrating superior strategy and valor.

1999

Kargil War

Successful Operation Vijay to evict Pakistani intruders from Indian territory in the Kargil sector.
