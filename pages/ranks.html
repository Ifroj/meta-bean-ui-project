Indian Army Ranks & Badges

The hierarchical structure of the Indian Army represents authority, responsibility, and honor. Each rank carries specific duties and privileges.

Commissioned Officers

Officers who hold a commission from the President of India. They lead from the front and command troops.

Field Marshal

5 Star Rank

Honorary rank, the highest attainable rank in the Indian Army. Only two officers have been conferred this rank.

Insignia: National emblem over a crossed baton and saber in a wreath

General

4 Star Rank

Highest operational rank, held by the Chief of Army Staff. Commands field armies and has significant strategic responsibilities.

Insignia: Crossed baton and saber with a star

Lieutenant General

3 Star Rank

Commands a corps-sized formation (20,000-45,000 troops). Often holds appointments like Army Commander or Vice Chief.

Insignia: Crossed baton and saber

Major General

2 Star Rank

Commands a division-sized formation (10,000-15,000 troops). Key appointments in headquarters and field formations.

Insignia: Five-pointed star

Brigadier

1 Star Rank

Commands a brigade-sized formation (3,000-5,000 troops). Senior staff appointments in various headquarters.

Insignia: Three five-pointed stars in a triangle

Colonel & Below

Field Officers

Includes Colonel, Lieutenant Colonel, and Major. Command battalions/regiments and hold key staff appointments.

Insignia: Various combinations of stars and stripes

Junior Commissioned Officers (JCOs)

Senior non-commissioned officers who act as a bridge between commissioned officers and other ranks.

Rank Insignia Role & Responsibilities Promotion Path
Subedar Major
Senior-most JCO, advisor to commanding officer, discipline maintenance After 26-28 years of service
Subedar
Platoon commander, training supervision, administrative duties After 20-22 years of service
Naib Subedar
Section commander, assists Subedar in platoon duties After 15-18 years of service

Other Ranks (ORs)

Soldiers who form the backbone of the Indian Army's fighting force.

Havildar

Section second-in-command, 12+ years service

Naik

Section commander, 8-10 years service

Lance Naik

Assistant section commander, 4-6 years service

Sepoy

Basic soldier, fresh recruit to 3 years service
