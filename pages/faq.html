Eligibility Criteria

Age requirements vary based on the entry scheme:

• Agniveer (Soldier): 17.5 to 21 years (relaxable for某些 categories)
• Technical Entries: 20 to 27 years for graduates
• NCC Special Entry: 19 to 25 years
• Women Entry: 19 to 25 years

Age relaxation is provided for certain categories as per government rules.

Educational requirements vary by entry type:

• Soldier (General Duty): Class 10th/Matric with 45% marks
• Soldier (Technical): 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Maths
• Officer Entries: Graduation degree (varies by branch)
• Technical Entries: Engineering degree in relevant field

Specific requirements are detailed in each recruitment notification.

Minimum physical standards:

• Height: 157.5 cm for men, 152 cm for women (relaxable for某些 regions)
• Chest: 77 cm (minimum), 82 cm (expanded) for men
• Weight: Proportionate to height and age

Regional relaxations apply for candidates from certain areas like North-East, Hilly regions, and Tribal areas.

Recruitment Process

The Agniveer selection process includes:

1. Online Registration - Application submission
2. Physical Fitness Test - 1.6km run, pull-ups, etc.
3. Physical Measurement - Height, weight, chest
4. Medical Examination - Comprehensive health check
5. Written Test - Common Entrance Examination
6. Document Verification - Original certificate check

Successful candidates undergo 6 months of training.

Officer entries have different application processes:

• NDA/NA - Through UPSC examination
• CDS - Combined Defense Services exam by UPSC
• Technical Entries - Direct application on army website
• NCC Special Entry - For NCC 'C' certificate holders
• JAG Entry - For law graduates

Each entry has specific eligibility and application procedures.

Essential documents include:

• Class 10th and 12th mark sheets and certificates
• Graduation degree and mark sheets (for officer entries)
• Domicile certificate
• Caste certificate (if applicable)
• NCC certificate (if applicable)
• Sports achievement certificates (if applicable)
• Identity proof (Aadhar card, voter ID, etc.)
• Recent passport-size photographs

All documents must be original and self-attested copies.

Training & Preparation

Training duration varies:

• Agniveer: 6 months basic training + 6 months advanced training
• Soldiers: 9 months to 1 year (depending on trade)
• JCOs: 1-2 years (including promotion courses)
• Officers (IMA): 18 months
• Officers (OTA): 49 weeks

Specialized training durations vary based on the arm/service.

Recommended preparation plan:

• Running: Start with 2km daily, gradually increase to 5km
• Pull-ups: Practice daily, aim for 10+ repetitions
• Push-ups: Minimum 30 in 2 minutes
• Sit-ups: Minimum 40 in 2 minutes
• Obstacle Course: Practice individual obstacles
• Diet: High protein, balanced nutrition
• Rest: Adequate sleep and recovery

Follow the training guides available on our fitness page.

Officer training includes:

• Military Subjects: Weapon training, tactics, map reading
• Leadership: Command and staff duties
• Physical Training: Endurance, strength, obstacle courses
• Academics: Military history, law, administration
• Adventure: Mountaineering, watermanship, parasailing
• Field Craft: Camouflage, field defenses, patrolling
• Drill: Ceremonial drill and parade training

Training is comprehensive and transforms civilians into leaders.

Career & Benefits

Career progression varies by entry:

• Agniveer: 4-year service, 25% retained for regular cadre
• Soldiers: Can rise to JCO ranks through promotion
• JCOs: Can become Honorary Commissioned Officers
• Officers: Can rise to General ranks based on merit

Promotion is based on merit, seniority, and vacancies. Specialized courses and higher education opportunities are available.

Comprehensive benefits include:

• Pay & Allowances: Basic pay, DA, HRA, Transport allowance
• Medical: Free medical care for self and dependents
• Housing: Accommodation or HRA
• Ration: Subsidized rations
• Leave: Annual leave, casual leave, sick leave
• Education: Children education allowance
• Travel: Leave travel concession
• Pension: For regular cadre after retirement
• Insurance: Group insurance schemes

Yes, the army encourages higher education:

• Study Leave: Up to 2 years for higher studies
• Correspondence Courses: Through IGNOU and other universities
• Professional Courses: Technical and management courses
• Foreign Courses: Selected officers can study abroad
• Financial Assistance: For approved courses

Many officers complete Master's degrees, PhDs, and professional qualifications while serving.
